<p>The <b>Skeletal System</b> is made of all of the bones in the body.  It protects the <a href="/wiki/Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Reproductive_System" title="Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Reproductive System">reproductive organs</a>, and vital organs like the <a href="/wiki/Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Circulatory_System" title="Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Circulatory System">heart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Respiratory_System" title="Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Respiratory System">lungs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Nervous_System" title="Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Nervous System">brain</a>, and is a place for <a href="/wiki/Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Muscular_System" title="Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Muscular System">muscles</a> to attach.  Bones are a very important part of the human body.  Without them, we would simply collapse, and be very unstable.  They support all of our tissue and muscles, and they are very difficult to break.
</p><p>The inside of the bones is called the <i>bone marrow</i>.  This is where most <a href="/wiki/Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Circulatory_System" title="Wikijunior:Biology/Systems/Circulatory System">blood cells</a> are made.
</p><p>Humans are born with about 300 bones.  During childhood, some of these bones fuse together, so that an adult human has a little more than 200 bones.
